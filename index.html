I Am Now Ray Nielson: A Rebranding Effort in Beige

By Formerly-Fred-McAlpine-Now-Ray-Nielson

Look, I’ve had enough. Enough of the smoothie-handed identity workshops, enough of the no-nipple techno-feudalism, enough of Glastonbury lineups that sound like they were generated by a bored AI trying to impress a sociology professor on mushrooms.

My name was Fred McAlpine.

Fred: a name you give your uncle, or a Labrador.
McAlpine: sounds like a DIY chain that sells screws and grudges.

No more.

From this point forward, I shall be known as Ray Nielson.

Why? Because Ray sounds like a man who wears socks with sandals and doesn’t apologise for it. And Nielson? That’s data, baby. That's statistically significant. That’s a man who’s done the charts, compared the vibes, and realised: being Fred is no longer culturally relevant.

The State of the Union (of Me)

This declaration comes after attending Euro Trip: Beach Vibz—yes, spelt with a “z,” like a teenager trying to win at Scrabble with vibes alone. It was meant to be a relaxing escape, but felt more like a soft dystopia designed by Gwyneth Paltrow and enforced by an algorithm in hemp trousers.

First off, nipples are banned.
You heard me. Outlawed. The human body, apparently, is no longer EU-compliant. I stood there with my smoothie (which tasted like gentrification in liquid form), wondering if I’d accidentally joined some sort of Enlightenment Cult With Sand. I half-expected Rousseau to emerge from the sea wearing biodegradable crocs and whisper, “Authenticity is performative, bro.”

At some point, a teenager handed me a beige frump bikini top and told me it would “stabilise my energy field.” I think what they meant was “cover your offensive chest raisins before someone has a vibrational seizure.” I complied, of course. I’m not made of £200 refunds.

But the whole thing got me thinking — not just about my nipples, but about everything. The cultural moment. The symbolic decline of Snow Patrol. The way British comedy has been quietly dragging society through existential therapy with nothing but sarcasm and uncomfortable silences.

So I’m done pretending. I’m not Fred anymore. Fred wrote about bands. Fred tried to laugh through the pain. Ray knows the pain is the bit.

Political Theory Corner (w/ Dad Jokes)

Let’s bring some structure to the chaos. I’m a middle-aged man with a new name, a mankini tan line, and a head full of Gramsci. So buckle up.

Neoliberalism is a Beach Club Now

What I experienced wasn’t a holiday—it was late-stage capitalism in biodegradable swimwear. You’re not relaxing, you’re curating your wellness aura™. It’s not leisure; it’s content farming. Foucault warned us about the prison of self-discipline—he just didn’t know it would come with complimentary smoothies.

The Nipple Ban is Postmodern Hegemony

In a world where nipples cause vibrational disruption, we have truly exited the realm of the real. Baudrillard would’ve had a coronary. The map is now the smoothie, the territory is your shame, and both are available for £200 per person with a loyalty card.

Snow Patrol is the Last Bastion of Authenticity

I said what I said. While “The Smell of Your Lips” strums ambiguous chords about seaweed feminism, Snow Patrol is out here delivering actual melodies with emotional integrity. Chasing Cars is pure, raw ontology with a soft intro.

British Comedy is the Real Leftist Project

Forget the Labour Party. The real class warriors wear mismatched suits and mumble about bin day. We don’t need revolution; we need Peep Show reruns and James Acaster whispering that everything’s awful but at least it’s symmetrical.

The Rebrand Strategy

So yes, I’m rebranding. Like Twitter into X but with fewer conspiracy theories and more body dysmorphia.

I am Ray Nielson now.
I’ve updated the LinkedIn.
I’ve burned my Crocs (the ones that weren't biodegradable).
I’m leaning into my mediocrity with dignity and SPF 50.

Ray Nielson does not chase vibes. Ray is the vibe. A beige, dad-joked, Gramscian vibe that wants the world to know: just because everything’s performative doesn’t mean it can’t also be incredibly lame.

Final Thoughts From the Frump Zone

To anyone else out there spiralling through midlife rebranding, know this: your mankini of shame might just be the chrysalis from which your truest self emerges.

Maybe, just maybe, the smoothie was inside you all along.

Now if you’ll excuse me, I’m off to design a one-man fringe show called "Vibrational Consequences: The Ray Nielson Manifesto." Featuring interpretive dance, some Snow Patrol, and a TED Talk about nipple politics.

Because that’s who I am now.
